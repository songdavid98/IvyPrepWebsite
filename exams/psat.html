What is the PSAT?

"The Preliminary SAT/National Merit Scholarship Qualifying Test (PSAT/NMSQT) is a standardized test administered by the College Board and cosponsored by the National Merit Scholarship Corporation (NMSC) in the United States. Approximately 3.5 million students take the PSAT/NMSQT each year. In 2013, 1.59 million high school sophomores and 1.55 million high school juniors took the PSAT. Younger students are also eligible to take the test. The scores from the PSAT/NMSQT are used to determine eligibility and qualification for the National Merit Scholarship Program." - From Wikipedia

The PSAT Sections

The PSAT has four sections: Reading, Writing and Language, Math (no calculator), Math (with calculator).

Reading

Topics include:

- World Literature

- Social Studies

- History

- Science

65 minutes / 47 Questions

Math

Topics include:

- Algebra

- Data Analysis

- Problem Solving

25 Minutes (No calculator)
45 minutes (With calculator)
47 Questions Total

Writing

Topics Include:

- Expression of Ideas

- Standard English Conventions

- Words in Context

- Command of Evidence

35 minutes / 44 Questions



How is it Scored?

1. By combining your scores on the three sections (Math, Writing/Language, and Reading), your final score will range from 320 to 1520.

2. Your score is determined by adding up your scores from the individual sections. Wrong answers are not penalized, so always guess!

3. Your raw score is then converted to a scaled score, which is then used to calculate your percentile rank which reports how well you did relative to other test takers. If you scored in the 80th percentile you scored higher than 80% of test takers
