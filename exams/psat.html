What is the PSAT?

The SAT is a test colleges and universities use to compare applicants and make admissions decisions. The test measures mastery of the skills and concepts learned in high school that are essential to success in college, the workplace, and beyond. We have revised our material to meet the challenges of the New SAT. The math secions test students’ ability to reason quantitively through real-life scenarios and arrive at practical decisions; the reading and writing sections test their ability to comprehend complex written texts and engage the content through evidence-based analysis and rhetoric. Our uniquely designed courses build on these skills and include strategies necessary to score in the 90% range. All of our sessions are taught by subject experts who have scored, at minimum, in the 95th percentile or higher on the SAT, and are experts in test-taking strategies.

How is it Scored?



By combining your scores on the three sections (Math, Writing/Language, and Reading),
your final score will range from 320 to 1520.


Your score is determined by adding up your scores from the individual sections.
Wrong answers are not penalized, so always guess!


Your raw score is then converted to a scaled score, which is then used to calculate your
percentile rank which reports how well you did relative to other test takers.
If you scored in the 80th percentile you scored higher than 80% of test takers
