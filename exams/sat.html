What is the SAT?


The SAT is a test colleges and universities use to compare applicants and make admissions decisions. The test measures mastery of the skills and concepts learned in high school that are essential to success in college, the workplace, and beyond. We have revised our material to meet the challenges of the New SAT. The math secions test studentsâ€™ ability to reason quantitively through real-life scenarios and arrive at practical decisions; the reading and writing sections test their ability to comprehend complex written texts and engage the content through evidence-based analysis and rhetoric.

The SAT Sections

The SAT is broken down into four sections: Reading, Writing and Language, Math, and an optional Essay.

Topics include:

- World Literature

- Social Studies

- History

- Science

Writing

Topics Include:

- Expression of Ideas

- Grammar

- Words in Context

- Command of Evidence

35 minutes / 44 Questions

How is it Scored?

1. Your score is determined by your performance of the two sections (Math / Reading and Writing), scored between 200-800 with a maximum score of 1600 .

2. Your score is determined by adding up correct answers. Wrong answers are not penalized, so always guess and never leave anything blank!

3. Your raw score is then converted to a scaled score, which is then used to calculate your percentile rank which reports how well you did relative to other test takers. If you scored in the 80th percentile you scored higher than 80% of test takers.

4. The optional essay is graded seperately. Although the essay is optional some schools require / recommend that you take the essay. Make sure you know whether or not you should take the essay before taking the SAT.
