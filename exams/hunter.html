What is the Hunter Entrance Exam?

The Hunter Entrance Exam consists of two multiple-choice sections covering English and Math and a writing assignment. Students have three hours to complete the Exam. It is given once per year, with no make-up dates or rescheduling to determine admission into the prestigious Hunter High School

The Hunter Entrance Exam Sections

The exam is broken down into three section: critical reading, math, and writing.

Critical Reading

This section will:

- Test your ability to understand, interpret and analyze different passages.

- Assess your reading comprehension skills

- Inculude a wide range of writing stlyes

- Questions related to the given passages

50 Multiple choice questions

Math

This section will:

- Test your problem solving ability

- Contain a variety of math problems

- Include multi-step questions

30 Multiple choice questions

Writing Assignment

This section will:

- Either require an Essay or a Creative piece

- Require you to demonstrate your orginality, efffienncy, and use of details

- Give a choice of topics or sometimes just one topic

- Provide scrap paper for outlines and drafts

2 Page Essay or Creative Piece

How is it Scored?

1. Based on the performance of all test takers a cut-off score is established. The top 500 scorers on the multiple choice sections will have their essay read by Hunter College High School teachers.

2. The essays are only identified by your HCHS identification numbers, meaning the readers do not know the identity of the students who wrote the essay at any point of the process.

3. From the 500 students, the students who write the top 170 essays will be admittted into HCHS, with 25 or so students put on the wait-list.
