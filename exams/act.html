What is the ACT?


The ACT is another standardized test that colleges and universities use to compare applicants and determine admissions. The ACT is designed to measure high school studentsâ€™ general educational development and their ability to complete college-level work. Unlike the SAT, the ACT is curriculum based so the questions on the ACT test the core subjects that high school students typically study through their third year of high school (English, reading, mathematics, and science). The test is accepted by all U.S. colleges and universities and can serve as an alternative to the SAT.

The ACT Sections

The ACT is broken down into five sections: English, Math, Reading, Science, and optional Writing.

English

Topics Include:

- Production of Writing

- Language Skills

- Understanding of English

- Rhetorical Skills

45 minutes / 75 Questions

Math

Topics Include:

- Pre-Algebra and Algebra

- Geometry

- Trigonometry

60 minutes / 60 Questions

Reading

Topics Include:

- Social Studies

- Reading Comprehension

- Literary Narratives

- Natural Sciences

35 minutes / 40 Questions

Science

Topics Include:

- Biology

- Chemistry

- Earth Science

- Physics

35 minutes / 40 Questions

Writing

Optional

Tests your writing skills, rhetoric skills, and evaluative argument skills

The essay prompt asks the student to address contemporary issues and gives 3 different perspectives. The student then needs to develop their own unique perspective and views based on the others

50 minutes (Optional)

How is it Scored?

1. Your score is determined by your performance on the four requried sections, scored between 1-36 each.

2. Raw scores on each section are convereted to scaled scores in 1 point increments, up to a total of 36 points.

3. Your Composite score is then calculated by averaging the four test scores.

4. On the ACT there is no penalty for wrong answers so always guess rather than leaving it blank.
